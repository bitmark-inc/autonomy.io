Web3

Web3 is a concept referring to the next iteration of the internet, the fundamental infrastructure of which relies on blockchain technology and peer-to-peer networks. The ethos of Web3 is to create a more equitable and decentralized system that empowers users with ownership over their online interactions and their data. A loosely defined and evolving term, Web3 encompasses NFTs, the metaverse, and DeFi, among other domains.

Blockchain

A publicly-accessible digital ledger used to store and transfer information without the need for a central authority. Blockchains are the core technology on which the Web3 ecosystem relies.

Decentralized

Blockchain technology ensures decentralization, meaning there is no primary governing figure or authority; rather, all interactions and operations are performed as a part of a distributed peer-to-peer network.

Metaverse

A theoretical and emergent concept for a “virtual world” that enables people to interact as they would in the physical world, accessed through digital interfaces such as mobile devices, computers and VR headsets.

Non-Fungible Token (NFT)

A unit that is totally unique as a digital asset that is certified on a digital ledger, or blockchain. An NFT can’t be exchanged with a like entity (the way one dollar bill is equivalent to another), so it can be used to digitally codify ownership of entities including art.

Fungible Token

A representation of a digital asset on the blockchain that is non-unique and can be traded or exchanged for another e.g. cryptocurrency.

Web3 Wallet

A digital wallet enables one to buy, trade and securely store digital assets, including non-fungible tokens and fungible tokens. Web3 wallets also act as one’s entry point into the larger DeFi and Web3 ecosystem, as one often needs a wallet to access Web3 applications.

Smart Contract

Self-executing code written into the blockchain that automates the execution of an agreement between two parties. Smart contracts enable NFTs to be sold and traded on the blockchain.

Generative Art

Art that is created in part or in whole with the use of an autonomous system or computer code and often relies on elements of randomness or chance e.g. Refik Anadol’s Unsupervised - Machine Hallucinations - MoMA.

On-Chain Art

A term referring to NFTs that “live” on the blockchain; in other words, both the smart contract tied to the NFT and the media content of the NFT (“the art”) is written into the blockchain. Most NFT’s are still stored off-chain.
